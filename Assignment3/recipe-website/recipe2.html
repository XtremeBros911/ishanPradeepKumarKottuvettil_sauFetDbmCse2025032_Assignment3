French Toast

Crispy outside, custardy inside. Breakfast perfection.

Non-Veg Easy Breakfast
Prep Time 5 min
Cook Time 10 min
Total Time 15 min
Servings 4 slices
Difficulty Easy

French toast is proof that the simplest ingredients can create something magical. Stale bread is not a problem here — it is actually preferred. The drier the bread, the more custard it soaks up, and the better the final result. This recipe uses a spiced egg-and-milk mixture that turns ordinary slices into a warm, fragrant, indulgent breakfast.

Ingredients

  • 4 thick slices of bread (brioche or white sandwich bread work best)
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons butter (for the pan)
  • Maple syrup and powdered sugar for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the custard. In a wide, shallow bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, cinnamon, sugar, and vanilla extract until the mixture is smooth and uniform. There should be no streaks of egg white visible.
  2. Soak the bread. Dip each bread slice into the custard mixture, letting it soak for about 15 to 20 seconds per side. You want the bread to absorb the mixture without becoming so saturated that it falls apart. Slightly stale bread handles this best.
  3. Heat the pan. Place a non-stick pan or flat griddle on medium heat. Add a tablespoon of butter and let it melt until it starts to foam slightly — that is when the pan is at the right temperature.
  4. Cook until golden. Place the soaked bread slices on the pan. Cook for 2 to 3 minutes on the first side, until the bottom is a deep golden brown. Flip carefully and cook the other side for another 2 minutes. The toast should feel firm to the touch but still slightly soft in the centre.
  5. Serve warm. Transfer to a plate, dust with powdered sugar, and drizzle generously with maple syrup. Add fresh strawberries or blueberries on the side if you have them.
Tip: Day-old bread works far better than fresh bread for French toast. Fresh bread tends to get too soggy in the custard. If you only have fresh bread, lightly toast the slices before dipping.
