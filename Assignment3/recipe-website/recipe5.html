Grilled Cheese

Golden, crispy, and impossibly cheesy.

Home Pancakes French Toast Omelette Pasta Grilled Cheese
Veg Easy Snack
Prep Time 3 min
Cook Time 8 min
Total Time 11 min
Servings 1 sandwich
Difficulty Easy

A grilled cheese sandwich is deceptively simple. Two slices of bread, butter, and cheese — that is it. But the difference between an okay grilled cheese and a great one comes down to technique: low-and-slow heat for an evenly golden crust, the right cheese for maximum melt, and patience. Get those three things right, and this humble sandwich becomes something genuinely special.

Ingredients

  • 2 slices of white or sourdough bread
  • 2 to 3 slices of cheddar cheese (or a mix of cheddar and mozzarella)
  • 1 tablespoon butter (softened, for spreading on the bread)
  • A pinch of garlic powder (optional)
  • A pinch of dried oregano (optional)

Instructions

  1. Butter the bread. Take the two slices of bread and spread softened butter generously on one side of each slice. This is the side that will be in contact with the pan, so make sure the butter reaches the edges. If you like, sprinkle a pinch of garlic powder and oregano on the buttered sides.
  2. Assemble the sandwich. Place one bread slice butter-side down in a cold pan. Layer the cheese slices evenly on top. Place the second bread slice on top, butter-side facing up.
  3. Cook low and slow. Turn the heat to medium-low. Let the sandwich cook for 3 to 4 minutes. Resist the urge to press it down with a spatula — that squeezes out the melted cheese. You will know it is time to flip when the bottom is an even, deep golden brown.
  4. Flip and finish. Carefully flip the sandwich using a wide spatula. Cook the other side for another 3 to 4 minutes. The cheese should be fully melted and starting to ooze slightly at the edges.
  5. Serve immediately. Transfer to a cutting board, let it rest for 30 seconds (the cheese will set slightly and make for a cleaner cut), then slice diagonally. Pair with a warm bowl of tomato soup for the ultimate comfort meal.
Tip: For an extra-crispy crust, try spreading a thin layer of mayonnaise on the outside of the bread instead of butter. It sounds unusual, but it creates an incredibly even, golden crust every time.
