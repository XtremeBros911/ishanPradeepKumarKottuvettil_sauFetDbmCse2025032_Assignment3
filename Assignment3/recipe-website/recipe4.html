One-Pot Pasta

One pot. Twenty-five minutes. Zero compromise on flavour.

Veg Easy Main Course
Prep Time 5 min
Cook Time 20 min
Total Time 25 min
Servings 2 to 3
Difficulty Easy

The beauty of one-pot pasta is in its simplicity. You throw everything into a single pot — dry pasta, fresh vegetables, garlic, olive oil, and water — and let it all cook together. The starch released by the pasta as it cooks creates a naturally creamy sauce without needing any cream. Less dishes, less effort, maximum flavour. This is the kind of recipe that earns a permanent spot in your weekly rotation.

Ingredients

  • 250g penne or rigatoni pasta (dry)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • A handful of fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • ½ teaspoon red chilli flakes
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 3 cups water (or vegetable broth for extra flavour)
  • ¼ cup grated parmesan cheese

Instructions

  1. Combine everything in the pot. Place the dry pasta, halved cherry tomatoes, sliced garlic, sliced onion, basil leaves, olive oil, chilli flakes, salt, and pepper into a large, deep pot. Do not turn on the heat yet.
  2. Add the liquid. Pour in the 3 cups of water or vegetable broth. Make sure the pasta is mostly submerged. It does not need to be fully covered — the liquid level will reduce as it cooks.
  3. Bring to a boil. Place the pot on high heat and bring the contents to a rolling boil. Stir once to prevent the pasta from sticking to the bottom.
  4. Reduce heat and simmer. Lower the heat to medium and let the pot simmer for about 12 to 15 minutes, stirring every 3 to 4 minutes. The pasta will absorb the liquid as it cooks. You will notice the sauce becoming thick and creamy — this is the starch doing its job.
  5. Check for doneness. Taste a piece of pasta. It should be al dente — tender but with a slight firmness in the centre. If the liquid has been absorbed but the pasta is not done, add a splash more water and continue cooking for another 2 minutes.
  6. Finish and serve. Remove from the heat, stir in the grated parmesan cheese, and let it sit for 2 minutes. The residual heat will melt the cheese into the sauce. Serve in warm bowls, topped with extra basil and a drizzle of olive oil.
Tip: For a richer sauce, swap one cup of the water with a cup of whole milk. Add it halfway through the cooking process. This gives the pasta a creamier, almost risotto-like texture.
