Classic Pancakes

Fluffy, golden, and ready in 20 minutes.

Veg Easy Breakfast
Prep Time 5 min
Cook Time 15 min
Total Time 20 min
Servings 8 pancakes
Difficulty Easy

There is something deeply satisfying about making pancakes from scratch on a lazy morning. No boxed mixes, no complicated steps — just a simple batter, a hot pan, and a few minutes of patience. These come out fluffy, golden, and perfect every single time.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ¾ cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter (plus extra for the pan)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Mix the dry ingredients. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt until evenly combined.
  2. Combine the wet ingredients. In a separate bowl, beat the egg, then add the milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Stir until smooth.
  3. Make the batter. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients. Stir gently with a spatula until just combined. A few small lumps are perfectly fine — overmixing will make the pancakes tough.
  4. Heat your pan. Place a non-stick pan or flat griddle on medium heat. Add a small knob of butter and let it melt, swirling to coat the surface evenly.
  5. Cook the pancakes. Pour roughly ¼ cup of batter onto the pan for each pancake. Cook until you see small bubbles forming on the surface and the edges begin to look set — about 2 to 3 minutes. Flip carefully and cook the other side for another 1 to 2 minutes until golden brown.
  6. Serve immediately. Stack the pancakes on a warm plate. Top with a pat of butter, a generous drizzle of maple syrup, and fresh berries if you like. Serve while still hot.
Tip: Let the batter rest for 5 minutes before cooking. This gives the baking powder time to activate, resulting in even fluffier pancakes.
