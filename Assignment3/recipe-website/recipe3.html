Masala Omelette

The classic Indian street-style egg, loaded with spice.

Home Pancakes French Toast Omelette Pasta Grilled Cheese
Non-Veg Easy Breakfast
Prep Time 5 min
Cook Time 5 min
Total Time 10 min
Servings 1 omelette
Difficulty Easy

If you have ever eaten at a roadside stall in India, you know this omelette. It is fast, spicy, and deeply satisfying. Unlike a delicate French omelette, the Indian masala version is cooked a bit longer so the outside gets lightly crispy, while the inside stays packed with crunchy onions, juicy tomatoes, and the sharp heat of green chillies. Pair it with buttered toast or a paratha, and you have a meal that punches well above its weight.

Ingredients

  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 small tomato, finely chopped
  • 1 to 2 green chillies, finely chopped (adjust to your heat preference)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • ¼ teaspoon red chilli powder (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon oil or butter

Instructions

  1. Prep the vegetables. Finely chop the onion, tomato, green chillies, and coriander leaves. Keep them ready on a plate — this recipe moves fast once the pan is hot.
  2. Beat the eggs. Crack the eggs into a bowl. Add the turmeric, red chilli powder, and salt. Beat vigorously with a fork until the yolks and whites are fully combined and the mixture looks uniform and slightly frothy.
  3. Add the vegetables to the eggs. Fold the chopped onion, tomato, green chillies, and half of the coriander into the egg mixture. Stir once or twice to distribute everything evenly.
  4. Cook the omelette. Heat oil or butter in a non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Once the oil starts to shimmer, pour in the egg mixture and spread it evenly across the pan. Let it cook undisturbed for about 2 minutes, until the bottom is set and golden. Flip and cook the other side for another minute or so.
  5. Serve hot. Slide the omelette onto a plate, garnish with the remaining coriander, and serve immediately with buttered toast or a warm paratha on the side.
Tip: For extra crispiness, sprinkle a pinch of rice flour or cornstarch into the egg mixture before cooking. It gives the omelette a slightly crunchier texture on the outside without changing the flavour.
